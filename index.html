SpeakBirth

Designing for Black maternal survival, an app to help Black mothers find culturally competent providers, navigate insurance, and warn each other about unsafe care.

By Nailah Lewis · Fall 2026
About this project

A class project on a real disparity

Black women in the United States are more than three times as likely to die from a pregnancy-related cause than white women. The Kaiser Family Foundation estimates 87% of those deaths are preventable. SpeakBirth is a mobile app concept built around three of the gaps that contribute to that disparity: finding culturally competent providers, navigating insurance benefits, and reading honest equity-focused reviews of hospitals.

This portfolio documents the full design process from problem framing through prototype and heuristic evaluation. Each phase applies core HCI principles: user-centered design, iterative refinement, and evidence-based evaluation, to a real-world problem worth taking seriously.
